The Future of Digital Archeology is worth visiting

Eventually I Would like to build a website for the company, this website would have all of our services with pricing and some examples of previous work we have done, this website would be accessible to all.

Now lets from here on out pretend that we are in the year 2021 and this company is a reality. We would add an audio portion to the website to allow people who have visual problems or other people who would like a audio guide rather then a self guide.

Also a holographic aspect for those wanting to submerse themselves within the website. This website would have five main pages, the first would be the about us page. This page would have an about Digiarch section on it, a place that shows some of the different people that of used us.

Also it would have an about the founder section. The second page would be the services page, this page would list out all of the services that we have to offer here with out company.

It would also house a section to show how different people are using our services in creative ways already, this would hopefully spark people imagination into different ways that they can use our services.

The third page would be a gallery page, this would house things that we would make out of the formats like Picktochart, and some of my word collages from Voyant, also just pictures of different sites that we have worked on over the years.

The fourth page would would be a contact page so that if anyone had any questions about any part of our website they would be able to ask away! The fifth page would include an virtual ethics book.

This is because we would want our clients to know that we are up to date in the ethics department so they know that we will be able to provide the findings with ethical results. The last page would be our wall of fame, this would be a section to honour all of our big spenders.

Anyone who did a long term project with us and wanted to be listed on our site and recognized. The site would also be rittled with different pictures of all of the work we have done in hopes to wow people!

Tools for Digital Archaeology III

Mapbox would also be a crucial part to the website, with this Mapbox tool the people scanning through our website could quickly look to find previous work that we have conducted all over the world to see the actual location of these projects, for if you are close by you can even go look at one of our finished projects.

Tools for Digital Archaeology IV

Twine would also be a great add to the website, this would allow people to scan through the different services that we offer through our company. They could click on a different service and it would take them to different ways to perform that task, from there you can go and look at the actual price of those tasks.
